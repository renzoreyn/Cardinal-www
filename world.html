World model

Everything is just terrain until agents start treating it like something more.

Environment

Persistent spatial simulation

Starts small: bounded 2D grid or navmesh, valley-scale region (~2km x 2km).

Scales over time to ~10km x 10km continent. Expansion is gated by agent population density and exploration behavior. The world doesn't grow until something pushes into it.

  • Forests
  • Rivers
  • Mountains
  • Plains
  • Caves
  • Coastlines
  • Dangerous zones

Environment fields

Time-dependent pressure

R(x, y, t)

Resource density. Regenerates slowly. Depletes under sustained use.

T(x, y, t)

Temperature. Varies by season cycle. Cold stress, heat stress.

C(x, y)

Traversal cost across the terrain mesh.

H(x, y, t)

Hazard probability. Static environments don't create pressure.

Seasonal cycle

They just notice food gets harder

1 real hour = 1 simulated day 1 real month = ~1 simulated year (4 seasons)

Agents don't know what seasons are. They just notice that food gets harder to find, and it gets cold, and some of them don't survive it.

That's the point.

Seasons affect

  • Resource availability
  • Temperature fields
  • Migration incentives
  • Reproduction timing

Degenerate world states

Failure modes worth tracking

Collapse

Resource collapse

Sustained overharvesting depletes a region permanently. Agents either migrate or die. This is not a bug.

Hazard

Dead zone formation

Areas permanently hazardous through event accumulation: fire spread, disease, territorial conflict residue.

Geography

Isolation pockets

Geography that cuts off agent groups, potentially forcing independent development.
