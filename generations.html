Generations

Children are not extensions of parents. They are new systems starting from near-zero.

Reproduction

Under conditions, not command

Minimum trust threshold. Adequate resource surplus. Shelter present. Safety threshold met. Social bonding duration. Agents can't reproduce on first contact.

The world doesn't go easier on children. That's intentional.

trait_child = weighted_avg(trait_parent_a, trait_parent_b) + mutation_noise

Children inherit

  • Genetic trait subset + mutation
  • Zero episodic memory
  • Weak procedural priming
  • Same survival pressure

Institutional inheritance

Behaviors that didn't break

Settlements

Repeated return to a location. It becomes home.

Leadership

High trust + resource success. Proto-authority.

Norms

Behaviors punished or rewarded consistently.

Trade routes

Exchange paths that survive across agents.

Rituals

Repeated group behaviors with no direct survival function.

Transmission is always lossy. A technique passed through five agents is not the same technique that started. This is a feature.
