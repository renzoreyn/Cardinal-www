System philosophy

Cardinal is not a scripted simulation. It doesn't contain "systems" in the traditional sense of games or simulations.

Pressure, not plot

If those appear, they're not implemented

No predefined governments, professions, morality systems, economies, technology trees, or social hierarchies.

They emerge.

Everything comes from pressure: resources, geography, memory, survival, interaction between agents.

Agents are not smart. The system is.

Not handed to them

  • Governments
  • Professions
  • Morality systems
  • Economies
  • Technology trees
  • Social hierarchies

Complexity

Shallow by design

Complexity emerges from interaction, not from individual agent intelligence.

The policy is intentionally shallow: no lookahead planning beyond 2–3 steps, no abstract goal representation, no concept of "self" beyond internal state pressure.

A_t = policy(S_t, O_t, M_t)
S_t = internal state (needs + tendencies)
O_t = current observation (local perception)
M_t = memory state
A_t = chosen action

Weighted heuristic selection in early versions. Not a neural network. The agent that's starving doesn't plan. It forages.

Future consideration

Descendants are shaped by what survived

Introducing a learned policy layer (via lightweight RL or imitation from social observation) for agents in later generations that have accumulated cultural exposure.

First-generation agents are pure heuristic. Descendants are shaped by what survived.
